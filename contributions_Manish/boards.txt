 === Manish Maps Explained ===

1. Snoopy Christmas Map (snoopy_christmas)
   64x59 squares.
   The win length is 5.
   Because its so big, bots that scan the whole board might get slow. But bots like HunterBot that only look at neighbors have a huge advantage here. This forces students to make smarter bots that dont waste time looking at empty space.

   Disadvantages for current bots:
   - Random/SmartRandom: The board is huge. Random moves are like finding a needle in a haystack. They will almost never connect 5 in a row by luck.
   - WalkingBot: It blindly fills the board row by row. It will spend the whole game playing in the top-left corner while the opponent wins easily somewhere else.
   - DiagonalBot: Similar to WalkingBot, it just fills diagonals starting from the corner. It ignores the opponent completely, which is a death sentence on a big map.
   - HunterBot: It's smarter because it looks near other pieces, but it can get "tunnel vision". It might focus too much on one small battle and miss a threat happening on the other side of the board.

2. Portal Map (portal)
   15x15 squares.
   This map is technically divided into 5 separate rooms: 4 small corners (3x3) and 1 big center (5x5).
   The win length is 4.
   The trick to win: You CANNOT win inside a corner room because they are only 3 wide.
   To win, u MUST play in the center room OR connect a line from a corner into the center through the tiny 1-block gaps ("portals").
   So the only way to win is to control the portals or the center.

   Disadvantages for current bots:
   - Random/SmartRandom: They are doomed. They will happily play in the corner rooms forever, not realizing they can never get 4 in a row there because the rooms are only 3x3.
   - WalkingBot: It starts at the top-left corner (0,0). That corner is a 3x3 room. It will fill that room, realize it can't win, and then just keep filling rows. It has no idea about the "portals".
   - DiagonalBot: It loves diagonals, but the corner rooms are too small for a diagonal win (max length 3). It will likely get stuck hitting walls inside the small rooms.
   - HunterBot: It only looks at neighbors. If the game starts in a corner, it might get stuck fighting in that small room. Unless someone plays near a "portal", it might never realize there is a whole center room to play in.

3. Ginger Bread Cookies Map (CustomBoard)
   35x102 squares.
   This map is very tall and narrow, filled with scattered obstacles.
   The win length is 5.
   
   Disadvantages for current bots:
   - WalkingBot: It scans row by row. With so many obstacles, it will waste many turns trying to play in spots that are already blocked or have no potential for a line.
   - DiagonalBot: This map is a nightmare for diagonals. The obstacles break up long lines constantly. It will likely keep hitting walls.
   - Random/SmartRandom: They will just play randomly into walls or small gaps where they can't win.
   - HunterBot: It might do okay if it finds an open area, but it could also get trapped in a small "room" of obstacles. It needs to be careful not to get boxed in.
